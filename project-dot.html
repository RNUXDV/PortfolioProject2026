Case Study

DOT (Flagship)

Radial navigation + intentional engagement. AI-aware safeguards. A systems-first exploration of healthier digital behavior.

Role
Concept • UX Research • Interaction Design
Focus
Intentional engagement • Presence • Agency
Artifacts
Prototype • IA • Flows • Rationale

The Problem

Infinite scroll and engagement-first patterns make it too easy to drift into passive, compulsive behavior. DOT explores interaction patterns that pull people back into choice.

Goals

  • Replace mindless scrolling with deliberate, user-controlled navigation.
  • Create a social experience that rewards coherence, not validation loops.
  • Design AI-aware safeguards that support autonomy and safety.

Research & Insights

Users default to “easy”

Reducing friction matters, but DOT uses friction strategically to increase intention.

Sharing ≠ validation

DOT explores sharing inside smaller, curated circles to reduce social pressure.

Safety must be built-in

Safeguards work best when they’re transparent, optional, and non-intrusive.

Users want coherence

Design can reduce distortion by nudging toward clarity, reflection, and choice.

Interaction System

The core interaction model uses a central “self” and orbiting dots. Navigation is radial—not feed-based— so the user chooses where attention goes.

Center Dot

Personal hub—identity, intent, and a curated home space.

Orbit Dots

Interest-based portals (People, Music, Learn, etc.) that invite intentional entry.

Design Exploration

Prototype

A lightweight HTML/CSS/JS prototype validates layout, motion, and interaction states before deeper build.
