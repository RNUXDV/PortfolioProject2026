Flagship Case Study

DOT

A radial, user-curated social platform concept designed to replace doomscrolling with intentional discovery.

RoleUX / UI
FocusSystems + Interaction
OutputPrototype + Narrative

Problem

People are trapped in algorithmic loops that amplify outrage and shorten attention.

Hypothesis

Replace infinite scroll with radial navigation and user-owned “orbits” to make engagement intentional.

Solution

A center “user universe” with orbiting dots (interests, connections, AI guide), plus a ripple-based discovery model.

Next

Key Insights

The core observations that shaped DOT’s interaction model.

Engagement ≠ fulfillment

Infinite feeds reward compulsion (novelty + outrage), not intention.

Spatial interfaces slow people down

Radial selection encourages choice over reaction and creates a sense of place.

Ownership builds trust

When users control their “space,” the platform feels less manipulative and more personal.

Discovery should feel earned

Ripple-based discovery feels aligned; algorithmic injection often feels intrusive.

The DOT System

A modular system designed to replace infinite scroll with intentional discovery.

User Universe

The center dot is a stable home base that emphasizes agency and identity.

Orbit-Based Navigation

Users rotate and enter dots instead of scrolling endless feeds.

Ripple-Based Discovery

New people/content appear through meaningful interactions over time.

AI Dot as Guide

AI supports reflection and safety without optimizing for addiction.

What I’d Test Next

If DOT were to move beyond concept, these are the key questions I’d validate through usability testing and experimentation.

  • Does radial navigation reduce compulsive behavior?
    Compare session length, exit intent, and self-reported satisfaction against traditional feed-based interfaces.
  • Can users understand the orbit model without onboarding?
    Observe first-time interactions to see whether meaning is inferred through motion and spatial cues alone.
  • Do “ripples” feel meaningful or random?
    Test whether users perceive ripple-based discovery as aligned and intentional rather than algorithmic noise.
  • Does AI guidance feel supportive, not intrusive?
    Measure trust, comfort, and perceived agency when AI suggestions are present versus hidden.
