UX designer shifting into frontend — building systems-first interfaces that feel alive, intentional, and user-respecting.

Systems-first UX Frontend build Information architecture Interaction design

What I do

I turn messy product complexity into clear flows, clean UI, and shippable frontends — with attention to how users feel, decide, and move through a system.

  • UX: research synthesis, flows, IA, usability, interaction patterns
  • UI: design systems thinking, visual hierarchy, component consistency
  • Build: HTML/CSS/JS implementations that match the intended experience

How I work

I build by making the invisible visible: constraints, decisions, tradeoffs, and the reason behind the interface.

  • Systems before screens: map the structure, then design the moments.
  • Clarity wins: reduce cognitive load, remove friction, tighten copy.
  • Ship + iterate: prototype, test, refine — then build it for real.

What I’m building

My flagship concept is DOT — a radial, human-centered social platform exploring how interaction design can reduce doomscroll behavior and support presence and agency. I’m using it as a sandbox to connect product thinking, UX craft, and frontend implementation.
